02 · Vision
Early detection and prompt treatment of cancer can save hundreds of thousands of lives each year. India's mission is to extend community-based cancer screening to every town, village, and district — identifying risk before disease advances, equipping health workers with clinical intelligence, and ensuring no patient is lost to follow-up. Achieving this requires connecting field screening camps, diagnostic laboratories, clinical decision-makers, and patient health records into a single, accountable digital pathway.

Ministry of Health — NPCDCS Framework

NPCDCS — National Cancer Programme

Mandates community-level screening camps for cervical, breast, and oral cancers at PHC and sub-centre level — standardising protocols, training health workers, and extending reach to underserved populations across India's districts.

Ayushman Bharat — Health & Wellness Centres

Over 1.6 lakh Health & Wellness Centres tasked with delivering comprehensive primary healthcare — including preventive cancer screening — to populations in India's towns, villages, and remote communities.

ABHA / Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission

Every citizen's Ayushman Bharat Health Account provides a unique, portable digital health identity — the backbone for longitudinal health records that follow a patient from first screening through diagnosis and long-term follow-up, regardless of where care is received.

National Cancer Grid (NCG)

A network of 300+ cancer centres standardising evidence-based cancer care protocols across India. NCG early-detection guidelines directly inform the configurable screening protocols at the heart of this platform — ensuring clinical rigour at community scale.

03 · What We're Building

What we're building

Enable a single organization to run mass cancer screening camps across communities — linking every patient to their Ayushman Bharat health identity — have Lab Coordinators enter test results directly into the system, let an AI engine surface clinical intelligence overnight, and ensure every record is reviewed and authenticated by a Doctor before any next action is taken; all while maintaining a complete, longitudinal health record for each patient from first screening through to long-term follow-up.

Identity & Field Operations
MODULE 01

Patient Registry & Master Health Record Updated

P0 · MVP

Single source of truth for patient identity — now with ABHA interoperability for national health ID linkage.

  • ABHA Lookup: Search existing ABHA records by health ID, mobile, or Aadhaar — auto-populate demographics on a confirmed match
  • ABHA Registration: Register new patients with Ayushman Bharat Health Account via NHA API; receive and store ABHA ID as the primary interoperable identifier
  • Internal Patient ID (UUID + QR) generated for every patient; used as primary key and fallback when ABHA API is unavailable
  • Demographics, household/family linkage, complete medical history
  • Longitudinal record: every screening session, sample, result, AI stratification, Doctor review, and future treatment episode linked over time
  • Duplicate detection at registration — fuzzy match on name, date of birth, mobile number, and ABHA ID
  • Digital Patient Card with QR code — printable as PVC card, barcode sticker, or paper slip
  • Versioned digital consent capture with withdrawal handling
  • Admin-only audited patient search, merge, and deactivation tool
MODULE 02

Mass Screening & Camp Operations Updated

P0 · MVP

Mobile-first module for field technicians — now with patient home geo-tagging and predefined ward/area selection.

  • Camp/session creation: location, date/time, capacity, assigned technicians, session type (Community Camp / Home Visit)
  • Predefined Ward/Area Selection: Technician selects the ward/area from an admin-configured master at session start (or per patient for home visits); used for geographic coverage reporting
  • Patient Home Address Geo-Tag: When screening at a patient's home, the app captures GPS coordinates and stores them against the patient's home address record — enables map-based coverage analysis in analytics
  • Batch registration — manual entry, CSV import, ABHA lookup, or card re-scan for returning patients
  • Configurable questionnaire rendered from the active Screening Protocol (Module 06)
  • On-device risk score calculation based on questionnaire responses
  • Session summary dashboard: patients screened vs. declined, data quality indicators, technician activity log
MODULE 03

Sample Collection & Chain of Custody

P0 · MVP

Tracks every sample from collection through to lab result, with full chain-of-custody accountability.

  • Sample registration — type driven by the active protocol's test panel — linked to patient and screening record
  • Barcode/QR label generation, printable on vial and container stickers
  • Collection metadata: technician, timestamp, optional geo-tag
  • Handover workflow: digital confirmation, scan-based count reconciliation between field and lab
  • Status pipeline: Collected → Handed Over → Received by Lab → In Analysis → Result Entered / Rejected
  • Rejection handling: reason capture (contaminated, insufficient volume, wrong container, etc.), automatic re-collection task creation, notification to Lab Coordinator
Lab Result Entry & Validation
MODULE 04

Lab Coordinator Result Entry Interface New in v1.0

P0 · MVP

Internal web interface for Lab Coordinators to submit test results directly into the system.

  • Barcode / QR Scan to Identify Sample: Scanning the sample barcode retrieves the patient record and verifies eligibility — the system confirms the sample status is "Received by Lab" and the required sample type has been collected before allowing result entry
  • Protocol-Driven Dynamic Form: The result entry form is auto-generated from the active Screening Protocol's test panel — correct markers, units, and reference range hints are shown automatically based on the cancer type being screened
  • Per-marker result fields with inline validation: range checks, mandatory field enforcement, unit confirmation
  • Attach scanned or photographed lab report document (PDF / image) for archival
  • Submit for review — submitted results enter the Module 05 validation queue
  • Rejection entry workflow: record rejection reason, trigger re-collection task
  • Lab Coordinator session log maintained for accountability and audit trail
MODULE 05

Result Management & Validation

P0 · MVP

Ensures every submitted result is accurate, verified, and traceable before reaching the AI engine and Doctor.

  • Ingestion and normalization of results against protocol-defined markers and units (from M04 Lab Entry)
  • Automated range validation — out-of-range values flagged for Lab Coordinator attention
  • Critical/urgent result flagging: values exceeding critical thresholds bypass the normal queue and trigger immediate alerts to Lab Coordinator and assigned Doctor — no overnight wait
  • Lab Coordinator verification step before results are released to the AI stratification queue
  • Result correction and versioning — no silent overwrites; full audit trail with before/after values
  • Verified results archived to the patient's master longitudinal record
Clinical Intelligence — Protocol & AI Engine
MODULE 06

Screening Protocol & Rules Configuration Updated

P0 · MVP

The admin-facing configuration engine — defines what is screened for, how results are captured, and how the AI engine classifies risk. No code changes required.

  • Cancer Type Definitions: name, description, applicable demographics
  • Test Panel Definitions: markers/tests (e.g. PSA, CEA, CA-125, HPV-DNA, cytology), units, reference ranges — these directly drive the Lab Coordinator entry form in M04
  • Questionnaire Builder: configurable risk-factor questions with scoring weights
  • AI Classification Rules: threshold and multi-marker condition rules that produce Negative / Suspect classifications for the overnight AI engine (M07)
  • Protocol Versioning: protocols are immutable once activated; historical interpretation is preserved for audit purposes
  • Follow-up Schedule Templates: re-screening intervals per cancer type and outcome
  • Ward / Area Master: configurable list of wards, talukas, and areas available for Technician selection in M02
  • Admin simulation tool: preview AI classification outcomes against sample data before activating a new protocol version
MODULE 07

AI Risk Stratification Engine Updated

P0 · MVP

Overnight batch AI that analyses all verified lab results, classifies risk, and prepares an explainable intelligence summary for Doctor review. No auto-referral — advisory output only.

  • Overnight batch processing: runs on a configurable nightly window (e.g. 11 PM – 5 AM) over all results verified since the previous run
  • Applies the active Screening Protocol's classification rules to results, demographics, and questionnaire risk score
  • Classification output: Negative or Suspect, with a confidence score and the key contributing factors (markers triggered, thresholds crossed) — shown in plain language for clinical review
  • AI Intelligence Summary per case: a structured explanation of WHY the classification was reached, to aid Doctor decision-making — not a diagnosis
  • Critical/high-risk fast path: cases with critical marker values are flagged immediately (outside the batch window) for same-day Doctor alert
  • No auto-referral: AI output is advisory and informational only — all decisions require Doctor review and sign-off in Module 08
AI Classification Output: Negative or Suspect → Every case goes to Doctor Review (M08)
Clinical Review & Decision
MODULE 08

Doctor Review & Clinical Report Authentication Updated

P0 · MVP

Every case — Negative or Suspect — is reviewed and authenticated by a Doctor. Replaces the auto-referral pathway from v0.1. The Doctor's decision is the authoritative clinical record.

  • Doctor Auto-Assignment: Cases assigned to Doctors automatically based on configurable rules (workload-balanced, specialty, or facility). Manual re-assignment available to Admin.
  • Doctor Review Queue: Prioritized list of pending cases showing AI classification, critical flag (if any), patient summary, and time-in-queue
  • Case Review View: Side-by-side display of full lab results with reference ranges, AI Intelligence Summary (from M07), patient's complete screening history, and prior Doctor reviews
  • Clinical Observations & Notes: Free-text clinical notes and observations entered by the Doctor, stored as structured clinical narrative
  • Next Line of Action: Doctor selects from — Clear (Negative confirmed), Request additional investigation, Refer for Staging (Level 3), Refer for Day-Care Treatment (Level 2), Refer for Hospitalization (Level 1)
  • Prescription / Instructions: Doctor documents any immediate prescription, dietary advice, or patient instructions
  • Digital Signature Authentication: Doctor digitally signs and authenticates the completed report. The signed report becomes immutable and is stored as the authoritative medico-legal record.
  • Authenticated report triggers patient notification via SMS, Email, and/or WhatsApp with next steps in plain language
  • AI agreement/override logged: whenever the Doctor's final decision differs from the AI classification, the reason is captured for model feedback and audit

12 · For Discussion

Open Questions — MVP

These don't block the module structure, but resolving them early will sharpen detailed specifications and prevent rework.

Q1 ABHA API access & certification timeline
Has the organization applied for NHA ABHA API sandbox and production access? What is the expected certification timeline? What graceful degradation behavior is expected when ABHA API is unavailable during camps — should the system queue registration for retry when API connectivity is restored?
Q2 AI engine approach — rules vs. ML for MVP
For the overnight AI stratification (M07), should MVP use a pure rules-based engine driven by the Protocol configuration (thresholds and multi-marker rules), or is there an existing ML model/dataset to integrate from day one? Rules-based is recommended for MVP speed and allows the classification logic to be fully admin-configurable without a data science dependency.
Q3 Doctor digital signature mechanism
What digital signature standard applies? Options: PKI-based DSC (Digital Signature Certificate), OTP-based e-sign under India's IT Act Section 3A, or a platform-issued cryptographic hash with audit trail. This affects compliance with NMC guidelines for authenticated electronic medical records and may require legal counsel input.
Q4 WhatsApp BSP selection & template approval
Which WhatsApp Business Solution Provider (BSP) should be used — e.g. Gupshup, Kaleyra, Twilio, or ValueFirst? Are there existing contracts or preferences? Which message templates need to be pre-approved with Meta (registration confirmation, result notification, appointment reminder)? How many chatbot FAQ pairs are anticipated at launch?
Q5 Doctor auto-assignment rules
What logic should govern auto-assignment of cases to Doctors in M08? Options: round-robin within specialty, workload-balanced by pending case count, cancer-type specialist routing, or facility-based assignment. Who manages the assignment configuration — Admin only, or Department Heads as well?
Q6 Ward/Area master — source & granularity
Should Ward/Area definitions be manually entered by Admin, or imported from a government dataset (Census ward boundaries, Panchayat/ULB data, LGDMS)? How granular — District → Taluka → Ward → Village? This also determines the geographic analytics resolution in M13.
Q7 Consent capture method
On-screen signature pad on the mobile app, OTP-based digital consent, or printed-and-scanned consent forms? The choice must be legally defensible under the DPDP Act 2023 and align with clinical standards applicable to the screening program.
Q8 Regional language requirements
Which languages must SMS/Email/WhatsApp templates and chatbot responses support? Please prioritize by deployment geography (e.g. Marathi, Hindi, Gujarati, Kannada). Are translations provided by the organization or does the team need to arrange them?
Q9 Hardware for Lab barcode scanning
For Lab Coordinator barcode scanning in M04, is a dedicated USB/Bluetooth hardware scanner expected at each workstation, or will a webcam-based browser scanner suffice? For field printing, are there preferred or mandated printer models for the screening app (e.g. Zebra, Honeywell, Brother)?
Q10 Disaster recovery targets
What RPO (Recovery Point Objective) and RTO (Recovery Time Objective) are required for the secondary-region backup database? Should it be active-passive (failover) or active-active (read replica)? This determines infrastructure cost and the DR region.
