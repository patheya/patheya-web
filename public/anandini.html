01 · Foundational Decisions

What this platform is — and four decisions that shape it

📵

Patient-Facing Access

Phase 1–3: notification-only (SMS/Email). A full Patient Portal is planned as a later-phase module.

🏥

Treatment Pathway Depth

Full treatment management — Level 1/2/3 cycles, side effects, hospital integration — is the roadmap target, delivered in phases.

🧬

Cancer Type Scope

Generic & configurable. No cancer type, marker, or threshold is hardcoded — admins define protocols, test panels, and referral rules.

🏢

Tenancy

Single organization for now, with org/facility kept first-class so multi-tenancy can be added without a redesign.

02 · Vision

Why we're building this

Enable a single organization to run mass cancer screening camps across communities, seamlessly hand off samples to outsourced pathology labs, automatically interpret results against configurable clinical protocols, and guide every patient through the correct care pathway — from a negative result and routine follow-up, to Suspected, day-care treatment, or hospitalization — while maintaining a complete longitudinal health record for each patient.

Identity & Field Operations
MODULE 01

Patient Registry & Master Health Record

P0

The single source of truth for patient identity across multiple screening cycles and years.

  • Unique Patient ID (optionally mappable to a national health ID, e.g. ABHA)
  • Demographics, household/family linkage, full medical history
  • Longitudinal record: every session, sample, result, referral & treatment episode linked over time
  • Duplicate detection at registration (fuzzy match)
  • Digital Patient Card with QR code — printable as PVC card, barcode sticker, or paper
  • Versioned digital consent capture & withdrawal handling
  • Admin-only audited patient search & merge tool
MODULE 02

Mass Screening & Camp Operations

P0

Mobile-first module for field technicians running screening camps.

  • Camp/session creation: location, date/time, capacity, assigned technicians
  • Batch registration — manual entry, CSV import, or card re-scan for returning patients
  • Configurable questionnaire rendered from the active Screening Protocol (Module 06)
  • On-device, offline risk score calculation
  • Offline-first capture with background sync & conflict resolution
  • Session summary: screened vs. declined, data quality, technician activity
MODULE 03

Sample Collection & Chain of Custody

P0

Tracks every sample from collection to lab result, with full accountability.

  • Sample registration (type driven by the protocol's test panel), linked to patient & screening record
  • Barcode/QR generation, printable on vial/container stickers
  • Collection metadata: technician, timestamp, optional geo-tag
  • Handover workflow with digital signature & scan-based count reconciliation
  • Status pipeline: Collected → Handed Over → In Transit → Received by Lab → Analyzed → Result Received / Rejected
  • Rejection handling with reason capture & automatic re-collection task
Laboratory & Results
MODULE 04

Pathology Lab Integration

P0

Two-way integration with outsourced pathology lab partners.

  • Outbound: register sample + encrypted patient data, send handover notification, query status, request results
  • Inbound: receive results, sample-received confirmations, and rejection notices via webhook or polling
  • Adapter framework for multiple lab API formats — REST/JSON primary, CSV/file-drop fallback
  • Lab partner master data: registered labs, supported panels, SLA/TAT targets, credentials
MODULE 05

Result Management & Validation

P0

Ensures every result is accurate, verified, and traceable before it drives a referral decision.

  • Auto-ingestion & normalization against protocol-defined markers/units
  • Manual entry path for labs without API integration
  • Critical/urgent result flagging — bypasses normal queue for immediate alert
  • Clinician verification step before results reach the referral engine
  • Result correction & versioning — no silent overwrites, full audit trail
  • Archival linked to the patient's master record
Clinical Intelligence — Configuration & Referral Engine
MODULE 06

Screening Protocol & Rules Configuration

P0

New & central module. The admin-facing engine that defines what is screened for and how results are interpreted — without code changes.

  • Cancer Type Definitions: name, description, applicable demographics
  • Test Panel Definitions: markers/tests (e.g. PSA, CEA, CA-125, HPV-DNA, cytology), units, reference ranges
  • Questionnaire Builder: configurable risk questions with scoring weights
  • Referral Rule Engine: threshold/condition rules → Referral Level, incl. multi-marker rules
  • Protocol Versioning: immutable once used, preserving historical interpretation for audit
  • Follow-up Schedule Templates: re-screening intervals per cancer type/outcome
  • Admin simulation tool to preview referral outcomes before activation
MODULE 07

Risk Stratification & Referral Assignment Engine

P0

Turns verified results + patient context into an automatic, explainable referral decision.

  • Applies the active Screening Protocol's rules to results + demographics + risk score
  • Produces an auto-assigned Referral Level with confidence/score shown alongside
  • AI/ML scoring hook: rules-only at first; pluggable ML model integration reserved for later phases
  • Manual override workflow — mandatory justification, full audit trail, optional approval step for high-impact changes (e.g. Level 1 → Negative)
Referral Levels: Negative → Level 3 · Suspected → Level 2 · Day-Care → Level 1 · Hospitalization
Care Pathway & Treatment
MODULE 08

Referral & Care Pathway Management

P1

Covers the Negative pathway and Level 3 Suspected.

  • Negative: auto-notification, auto-generated next-screening date, case closed
  • Level 3 (Suspected): urgent notification to patient & clinician, oncology appointment booking, additional diagnostic ordering, TNM Suspected documentation
  • Decision gate → routes to Level 2 or Level 1
  • Referral history timeline — level changes, overrides, justifications
MODULE 09

Level 2: Day-Care Treatment Management

P1

Level 2 — early-stage, outpatient treatment.

  • Treatment plan creation/upload (type, goals, duration)
  • Cycle scheduling — chemo rounds, radiation sessions, drugs/doses, attendance
  • Investigation tracking: imaging, metastasis monitoring, tumor response
  • Side-effect monitoring with severity classification & automatic coordinator alerts for severe cases
  • Recovery classification (Complete/Partial/No Response) → continue, pivot, or escalate to Level 1
  • Discharge planning into post-treatment follow-up
MODULE 10

Level 1: Hospitalization Management

P1

Level 1 — advanced-stage, inpatient care.

  • Hospital/treatment center integration — API where available, manual fallback
  • Admission record: reason, clinical summary, ward/bed assignment
  • Inpatient tracking: transfers, surgery & complications, medications, transfusions, ICU status, daily vitals
  • Discharge: recovery assessment, summary, recommendations, routing to Level 2 or follow-up
MODULE 11

Post-Treatment & Survivorship Follow-up

P1

Keeps recovered patients on a long-term monitoring cadence.

  • Recovery monitoring schedule: weekly → monthly → quarterly, per protocol templates
  • Post-treatment imaging & tumor-marker monitoring schedules
  • Case closure workflow (declared recovered/stable)
  • Re-entry into the screening cycle for periodic survivorship screening
  • Overdue follow-up detection & escalation alerts
Oversight, Insights & Patient Engagement
MODULE 12

Case Management Dashboard

P0 / P1

Basic view at launch (P0); full real-time view in Phase 3 (P1).

  • Active cases list — filter/sort by referral level, status, facility, technician, overdue flag
  • Case detail: patient summary, full result history, referral history with overrides, treatment timeline, documents
  • Pending-action queue: awaiting results, appointment, treatment cycle, or follow-up
  • Real-time updates for sample status, critical flags, and overdue escalations
MODULE 13

Analytics & Reporting

P1 / P2

Basic campaign analytics first (P1); advanced insights & custom builder later (P2).

  • Screening campaign analytics: totals, result distribution, demographics, geography, time-to-result
  • Technician performance & lab partner performance (TAT, rejection rate, error rate)
  • Referral & treatment outcomes: auto-assignment accuracy, override rate, compliance, recovery rates
  • Compliance dashboard: consent completeness, follow-up adherence, audit exceptions
  • Standard + custom report builder; Excel/PDF export; scheduled email delivery to stakeholders
MODULE 14

Patient Portal

P2

A confirmed future-phase module, despite the notification-only starting point.

  • Secure OTP-based login tied to registered phone — accounts provisioned at registration, no self-signup
  • Dashboard: screening status, results in plain language, upcoming appointments, treatment overview
  • Result viewing & document download (reports, discharge summaries)
  • Appointment view/confirm/reschedule with reminders
  • Side-effect self-reporting feeding Module 09 monitoring
  • Educational content & FAQ links
06 · Shared Services

Cross-Cutting Modules

These underpin every module above and should be designed first as shared platform services.

🔐

CC1 — Identity, Access & User Management P0

  • OAuth2/OIDC (e.g. AWS Cognito), no self-signup
  • Role-Based Access Control across all roles
  • MFA for PHI-accessing roles
  • Facility/org-scoped permissions (multi-tenancy ready)
  • Full account lifecycle management
✉️

CC2 — Notifications & Communications P0

  • SMS & Email channels (no patient app needed)
  • Event-driven: registration, results, critical alerts, reminders, follow-up due
  • Templated, multi-language messaging
  • Delivery tracking & retry, India DLT-compliant SMS gateway
  • Scheduled stakeholder summary digests
🧾

CC3 — Audit Trail & Compliance Logging P0

  • Immutable audit log: PHI access, edits, overrides, config changes
  • Consent versioning & history
  • Retention/purge aligned to India's DPDP Act, 2023
  • Exportable compliance reports
🗂️

CC4 — Master Data & Configuration P0

  • Org/facility hierarchy — sites, camps, labs, hospitals
  • User/role master, location master
  • Links to Module 06 clinical configuration
  • System-wide alert thresholds & default schedules
📁

CC5 — Document & Media Storage P0

  • S3-compatible storage, India region
  • Consent forms, lab reports, scans, discharge summaries
  • Encryption at rest, signed-URL access via RBAC
  • Versioning & retention tagging
📡

CC6 — Real-Time Communication Layer P1

  • WebSocket/SSE for live dashboard updates
  • Sample status board, case alerts, critical results
  • Feeds Module 03 (sample tracking) & Module 12 (case dashboard)
📶

CC7 — Offline Sync Engine P0

  • Local-first storage (SQLite) on mobile
  • Background sync with conflict detection
  • Manual-review queue for unresolved conflicts
  • Sync status indicators for field staff
🖱️

CC8 — Hardware / Peripheral Integration P0

  • QR generation for Patient & Sample IDs
  • Barcode/sticker printing via Bluetooth/USB printers
  • PVC/hard-bound card printing workflow
  • Camera or hardware scanner for handover reconciliation

11 · For Discussion

Open Questions

These don't block the module structure, but resolving them early will sharpen the detailed design.

Q1 National Health ID integration
Should Patient ID generation attempt to link with India's ABHA (Ayushman Bharat Health Account) for interoperability, or remain fully internal?
Q2 Pathology lab API maturity
Does the intended outsourced lab partner(s) already expose REST/HL7 APIs, or will Phase 2 need a file-based/manual fallback as the primary integration initially?
Q3 AI/ML scope and data
Is the AI/ML component intended to be a rules-based scoring engine initially (Modules 06/07) with ML introduced later once sufficient historical data exists, or is there an existing model/dataset to integrate from day one?
Q4 Regional language requirements
Which languages must SMS/Email templates support for the target screening regions?
Q5 Hardware specifics
Are there preferred/mandated printer models or SDKs for QR/barcode sticker printing and PVC card printing that the Flutter app must support?
Q6 Consent capture method
On-screen signature pad, OTP-based consent, or printed-and-scanned consent forms?
Q7 Disaster recovery targets
What RPO/RTO are required for the backup database, and should it be active-passive or active-active?
Q8 Hospital partner readiness
For Module 10, will Level 1 hospitals provide API access (HL7 FHIR), or will hospitalization data be entered manually by your staff/liaisons?
