Executive Summary

One Platform. Every Farmer. Every Scheme.

The Opportunity

Maharashtra spends over ₹18,000 Crore annually across agricultural welfare schemes and has invested significantly in digital infrastructure — AgriStack / MHFR for Farmer Identity, MahaAgX for consent-based data exchange, MahaDBT for disbursement, and the Maharashtra Agri-AI Policy 2025–2029 as an active mandate with funded implementation tracks. The foundations are in place. What is needed now is the intelligence layer that connects these investments to farmer outcomes — ensuring the right benefit reaches the right farmer, proactively, every time.

The Shetkari Samruddhi Platform (SSP) is that intelligence layer. Built on top of Maharashtra's existing digital stack — not alongside it — SSP onboards every agricultural scheme's eligibility rules into a digital knowledge graph and matches them continuously against verified farmer profiles drawn from AgriStack and MahaAgX. An AI Eligibility Engine auto-identifies entitlements, notifies farmers proactively in Marathi, flags leakage for audit, and provides real-time governance dashboards for officials at every level.

SSP is a direct response to the Maharashtra Agri-AI Policy 2025–2029 Call for Proposals — a Track 2 submission with a working interactive prototype, positioned as the Scheme Coverage & Governance AI that completes Maharashtra's agricultural technology stack and delivers measurable outcomes from Month 6.

The Opportunity

The Gap Between Intent and Outcome

Maharashtra's agricultural welfare ambition is substantial — ₹18,000 Crore annually across 50+ schemes. The opportunity is to use AI to ensure every rupee reaches the right farmer, every eligible farmer is proactively found, and every rupee of recoverable leakage is surfaced for audit.

40%

40% of Eligible Farmers Await Proactive Outreach

An estimated 40% of eligible Maharashtra farmers are yet to enrol in one or more schemes they qualify for — not because they are ineligible, but because each scheme requires separate registration across different portals. A unified, AI-driven proactive outreach layer finds these farmers before they need to find the system.

₹1,500 Cr

₹1,500 Cr in Recoverable Savings — Detectable by AI

Ghost beneficiaries, deceased farmers still on rolls, duplicate registrations across districts, and ineligible claimants represent an estimated ₹1,200–1,500 Crore in annual leakage — invisible today because departmental databases are siloed. AI-driven cross-validation across AgriStack, Mahabhulekh, and DBT records can surface these patterns systematically.

1:1,200

Proactive Notification Multiplies the Extension Workforce

The extension officer-to-farmer ratio is 1:1,200 — every officer would need to individually inform 1,200 farmers of every new scheme. A digital proactive notification engine in Marathi via SMS, WhatsApp, and IVR effectively multiplies this reach to 1:1,200,000 — scheme awareness reaches every eligible farmer the day the scheme opens.

50+

50 Schemes — One Unified Intelligence Layer

Maharashtra administers over 50 central and state agri schemes across Agriculture, Revenue, Cooperation, Water Conservation, and Finance departments. Each holds its own beneficiary database. SSP's Scheme Knowledge Graph encodes all 50+ eligibility rule sets machine-readably — giving every stakeholder a single, unified view of farmer entitlements across the entire portfolio.

The Unifying Insight

All four gaps share a single solution: a unified intelligence layer that connects Maharashtra's existing identity infrastructure (AgriStack/MHFR), data exchange platform (MahaAgX), and disbursement engine (MahaDBT) with machine-readable scheme eligibility rules — enabling proactive, accurate, auditable scheme delivery at scale.

The Solution

Shetkari Samruddhi Platform (SSP)

A unified Digital Public Infrastructure that simultaneously builds verified farmer identities and digitises scheme eligibility — then uses AI to match, assign, and audit continuously.

Pillar A — Farmer Identity Stack (Demand Side)

  • 🪪 Farmer identity anchored on AgriStack / MHFR (mhfr.agristack.gov.in) — the National Farmer Registry already operational in Maharashtra; SSP extends, not replaces
  • 📋 Land profile seeded from Mahabhulekh (7/12, 8A) via MahaAgX consent layer — auto-updated on mutation
  • 🌾 Crop history from e-Crop, soil health card, PMFBY enrollment
  • 🏦 Financial profile — KCC, loan history, DBT bank account via Account Aggregator
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Household data — family composition, caste certificate, income certificate
  • 🔒 Consent management via MahaAgX — farmer controls which departments access what data, using AIAIC's live DPI consent framework
  • 📱 Accessible via Shetkari App, CSC kiosk, or assisted enrollment by Krishi Mitra

Pillar B — Scheme Knowledge Graph (Supply Side)

  • 📂 All 50+ schemes onboarded as machine-readable eligibility rule sets
  • 🔄 Live API sync with government portals — scheme rules updated automatically
  • 📏 Eligibility rules encoded: land ceiling, crop type, caste, income, geography
  • 🗂️ Scheme master data versioned — historical eligibility decisions are auditable
  • 💰 Benefit quantum and disbursement schedule linked to each scheme
  • 📅 Enrollment windows, deadlines, and document requirements tracked
  • 🔗 Cross-scheme dependency rules (e.g., PM-KISAN + Namo Shetkari additionality)
⚙️
The AI Matching Engine — Where Both Pillars Meet

The SSP AI Engine runs continuously, matching each farmer's verified profile against all scheme eligibility rule sets. It auto-generates entitlement recommendations, triggers enrollment workflows, notifies farmers of new eligibility in Marathi via SMS/WhatsApp/IVR, and simultaneously flags anomalies — deceased beneficiaries, land mismatch, duplicate accounts — for audit queues.

Challenges & Mitigations

Honest Assessment — Known Risks & How We Address Them

No transformative programme is without challenges. We have identified the most significant ones and built mitigations into the platform design from the outset.

🗂️ Data Quality in Mahabhulekh High Risk

Land records have errors, mutation backlogs, and digitisation gaps — particularly in Konkan and tribal areas. Seeding farmer IDs from poor-quality source data propagates errors.

Mitigation Probabilistic data quality scoring at ingestion; human-in-the-loop verification for low-confidence records; self-correction workflow for farmers via CSC kiosks. Prioritise high-quality districts for Phase 1.

🏛️ Inter-Department Data Sharing High Risk

Agriculture, Revenue, Finance, and Cooperation departments each guard their data. Without a Government Resolution mandating data sharing, SSP cannot access the source databases it needs.

Mitigation AIAIC to seek a Cabinet-level Government Resolution (GR) mandating data sharing. SSP designed on federated model — departments retain ownership; SSP accesses via APIs, not bulk copies. This preserves departmental sovereignty while enabling interoperability.

⚡ Aadhaar Seeding Gaps Medium Risk

Not all farmers — especially elderly, tribal, and tenant farmers — have Aadhaar-seeded bank accounts. SSP cannot auto-match or disburse without a verified bank linkage.

Mitigation Multi-modal identity: Aadhaar preferred but not exclusive. SSP accepts voter ID + land record combination as secondary identity. Enrolment camps run with BCs (Banking Correspondents) at CSCs for Aadhaar-bank seeding in parallel.

🤝 Change Management — Talathi Resistance Medium Risk

Automation of beneficiary selection removes discretionary power from Talathis and village-level workers. Resistance — including deliberate data entry errors — is a real deployment risk.

Mitigation Training programme for 15,000+ Talathis with incentive structure tied to data quality scores. Talathi dashboard designed to reduce burden, not just remove control. Phased rollout with feedback loops before state-wide enforcement.

⚖️ Political Sensitivity of Leakage Removal High Risk

Removing ghost beneficiaries who are politically connected, or who belong to organised farmer bodies, can create visible opposition. Mass de-enrollment without notice is a political liability.

Mitigation SSP flags anomalies for human review — it does not auto-delete beneficiaries. A structured notice period (30 days) is given for flagged beneficiaries to produce counter-evidence. "Exclude confirmed" only after review completion. This protects both the farmer and the government.

📶 Rural Connectivity Gaps Medium Risk

Real-time eligibility verification and notification requires internet connectivity that is unreliable or absent in many of Maharashtra's 40,000+ villages.

Mitigation Offline-first architecture for all field-facing tools. Changes sync when connectivity is available. CSCs used as connectivity hubs for villages without direct access. IVR fallback for all farmer notifications.

🔒 Data Privacy & Consent Medium Risk

Aggregating land, financial, and personal data in one platform creates privacy risk and potential for misuse if the consent and security framework is weak.

Mitigation Consent layer built into Farmer ID — farmers explicitly approve each data-sharing use case. Data encrypted at rest and in transit. Role-based access control so no single official can access the full farmer profile. Periodic privacy audit by independent body.

🔧 Legacy System Integration Medium Risk

APMC systems, state DBT portals, and district revenue systems run on different tech stacks — many without APIs. Integration requires significant custom connector work.

Mitigation API-first integration strategy with pre-built connectors for NIC-standard systems. Where APIs don't exist, scheduled batch sync via SFTP until systems are upgraded. Integration roadmap aligned with NIC Maharashtra's infrastructure modernisation timeline.

🔌 MahaAgX, AgriStack & MahaDBT API Maturity Medium Risk

SSP's intelligence layer depends on live API access to MahaAgX (data exchange), AgriStack/MHFR (Farmer ID), MahaDBT (disbursement status), and Mahabhulekh (land records). API SLAs, rate limits, and data freshness across these platforms vary and are not yet governed by formal integration agreements with a third-party system like SSP.

Mitigation Formalise platform integration MoUs with AIAIC (MahaAgX), NIC Maharashtra (AgriStack, Mahabhulekh), and Finance Dept (MahaDBT) as a pre-condition of Phase 1 — AIAIC's convening role makes this tractable. Implement resilient API wrapper layer with circuit-breaker patterns and scheduled batch fallback where real-time APIs are unavailable. SSP designed to degrade gracefully — partial data enables limited eligibility checks rather than total failure.

🏛️ Central Government Portal Dependencies Medium Risk

Scheme status for PM-KISAN, PMFBY, KCC, and Fertiliser DBT sits on Central Government portals — NIC-hosted systems not under Maharashtra's control. Direct API access requires MoA&FW or Ministry-level approval, which Maharashtra cannot unilaterally compel.

Mitigation PM-KISAN data accessed via farmer-consented DigiLocker pull (already available). PMFBY: Insurance Information Bureau APIs. Fertiliser DBT: iSAGaRD portal APIs. KCC: RBI Account Aggregator framework for bank-held data. Phase 1 scope restricted to state-controlled scheme data; central scheme integration progresses in Phase 2 with AIAIC's central government relationships as leverage.

Four Converging Reasons SSP Must Begin Now

1. Active Funded Policy Mandate — Track 2 Window Is Open

The Maharashtra Agri-AI Policy 2025–2029 is an active Call for Proposals. Track 2 (up to ₹2 Crore for pilot & validation) is open to mature solutions with working prototypes. SSP has a working interactive demo. AgriStack / MHFR is live. MahaAgX is live. The policy mandate, implementation infrastructure, and funding track are all in place — this is the moment to submit.

2. CAG Pressure is Increasing

The CAG's last performance audit of PM-KISAN and state agri schemes in Maharashtra flagged significant irregularities. The next audit cycle will be more stringent. SSP gives the government a credible, technology-driven response to CAG concerns before they become a political issue.

3. National Digital Infrastructure is Ready

DigiLocker, Account Aggregator, MOSIP, and PFMS — the national DPI stack SSP relies on — are now mature and proven. Three years ago, this platform would have required building these foundations from scratch. Today, they are ready APIs.

4. Every Month of Delay Has a Price

At ₹1,500 Cr in annual leakage, every 30-day delay costs the Maharashtra exchequer approximately ₹125 Crore. Every month without SSP also means thousands of eligible farmers miss a scheme enrollment window — a cost that cannot be recovered retrospectively.

The Decision That Changes Everything

SSP is not one more IT project. It is the infrastructure that makes Maharashtra's entire agricultural welfare system work as intended — reaching the right farmers, protecting public money, and generating the data needed to design better policy. The technical capability exists. The policy mandate exists. The financial case is unambiguous. What is needed is the decision to begin.
